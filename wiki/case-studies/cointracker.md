# CoinTracker: Surviving Crypto's Boom-Bust Cycles

CoinTracker (YC W18) is a crypto portfolio management and tax compliance platform. Co-founders Chandan Lodha and John built it to solve their own pain point: tracking crypto assets across exchanges and filing taxes. The company's journey illustrates how to navigate an industry defined by extreme cyclical volatility [1].

## Origin

Lodha and his co-founder had worked together previously and were interested in fintech. They were originally building something unrelated to crypto but became frustrated with traditional financial rails: "They're super slow and they're super inefficient" [1]. That frustration led them to explore crypto in 2017. Their first pain point was basic: "How the heck am I going to keep track of all these assets?" [1]. That kernel became CoinTracker's first product for portfolio tracking, later expanding to crypto tax compliance.

## The Cycle Problem

Crypto companies face a unique challenge. Bull cycles bring massive demand all at once; bear cycles can last years, during which "the entire world is telling us that the entire space that we're working in is irrelevant, dead, and doesn't make sense" [1]. Bitcoin alone has been declared dead over 470 times in the past decade.

Lodha frames the strategic question: "When there's excitement, how do we capture that excitement and push it, use it to propel ourselves forward? And when we're in a bear cycle, how do we hunker down and use our resources effectively to just continue building so that when that next wave of crypto adoption comes, we can ride that wave?" [1].

## The 2020 Near-Death Experience

In 2020, CoinTracker was six months away from shutting down. Lodha describes it as the lowest point of his professional life: "It's just a feeling of desperation of just like, oh my God, like nothing is working and there's nothing we can do about it" [1].

Two things got them through [1]:

1. **The co-founder relationship.** Lodha states: "I have an amazing co-founder and I cannot imagine having gone through this without him."
2. **First-principles thinking.** Rather than reacting to the local climate, they asked: does this mission make sense regardless of where we are in the cycle? Does it improve users' lives? They stuck to those first principles.

They also had direct, vulnerable conversations with the team: "We're not going to lie to you. Here is the reality of the situation. Here's what it means for us and here's what we need to accomplish in order to get back on a growth trajectory" [1].

## The Growth Trap

During DeFi summer in 2021, CoinTracker grew 14x. They focused on driving growth and acquiring users. But as the market pulled back, customer feedback revealed a problem: they had lost their product edge. The crypto ecosystem had evolved -- liquidity pools, Uniswap, advanced DeFi activity -- and CoinTracker had not kept up [1].

"It was a painful realization having users tell us that we weren't solving their problem anymore" [1].

## CoinTracker 2.0: The Nine-Month Rebuild

CoinTracker halted everything for nine months to reimagine the product and company. Two specific changes transformed how the company operated [1]:

### User Obsession

Everyone in the company began talking to users constantly: "From customer support to product design to marketing to the product management team to engineers." Lodha himself talks to users daily and monitors Reddit and Warpcast. "We want to be there. We want to understand what's working and what's not" [1].

### "Think Long-Term, Ship Today"

The company's guiding principle shifted from "think long-term" to "think long-term, ship today." This small change "gave everyone in the company the permission that they needed to take some risks and experiment and just build the thing that they wanted to build and try it out." The result: "The entire execution of the company massively accelerated almost overnight. People were shocked" [1].

## Lessons for Cyclical Industries

Lodha's core advice [1]:

- **Take a long-term view.** The companies that survive bear markets "get to reap the benefits of the really good times."
- **Stay humble when things go well.** During bull runs, do not lose product focus.
- **Stay believing when things are tough.** First-principles conviction in the mission matters more than the current market climate.
- **Use winters to rebuild.** Crypto winters are building seasons. CoinTracker used theirs to become the strongest product in the space by the time the next bull cycle arrived.
