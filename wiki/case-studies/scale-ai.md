# Scale AI

Scale AI, founded by Alexandr Wang as a YC company, evolved from a simple data labeling API into a multi-hundred-million-dollar infrastructure company powering America's AI ecosystem. In early 2025, Meta invested over $14 billion in Scale AI, valuing the company at $29 billion [1].

## Origin and Evolution

Scale AI started with a narrow premise: machine learning teams needed labeled data faster than they could produce it. Wang built an API for human labor -- a way to send raw data and get back labeled training examples. Behind the scenes, the real challenge was turning "messy, real-world data into something AI could learn from" [1].

The company evolved through several phases:
1. **Data labeling API**: The original product, serving ML teams at individual companies
2. **Enterprise data infrastructure**: Powering training data pipelines for Fortune 500 companies
3. **Government and defense**: Real-time military planning and intelligence analysis
4. **Agent infrastructure**: Building the evaluation and training infrastructure for the next generation of AI systems [1]

## Competing with China

Wang is one of the most vocal voices in Silicon Valley on US-China AI competition. He argues that data quality and infrastructure -- not just model architecture -- are the critical battleground. Chinese labs (DeepSeek, Qwen) have demonstrated that scaling laws work regardless of where you train, making the quality of training data and the speed of iteration the differentiators [1].

## Lessons for Founders

- **Start narrow, expand into the platform**: Scale began with a single API endpoint and expanded into the infrastructure layer that frontier AI depends on
- **Government can be a startup customer**: Scale's defense contracts demonstrate that government agencies can move at startup speed when the technology solves an urgent problem
- **The 19-year-old founder can build a $29B company**: Wang started Scale AI at 19, reinforcing the [[Starting Young]] thesis [1]
