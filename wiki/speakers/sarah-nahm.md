# Sarah Nahm

Sarah Nahm is the co-founder and CEO of [[Lever: Building Recruiting Software from the Inside Out|Lever]] (YC S12), a recruiting software company. She studied design at Stanford, worked at Google (as a speech writer for Marissa Mayer and on the Chrome team under Sundar Pichai), and transitioned from individual contributor designer to CEO on the eve of Lever's Series A in October 2014 [1].

## Worldview

Nahm's philosophy centers on three ideas:

**People before product, co-founders before ideas.** Nahm did not leave Google to start a company. She left to grow, and the co-founding team "were almost trying out working with each other for a while" before committing. When asked what matters most for getting started, she ranks: first, believing in the people you are working with; second, believing in a change happening in the world; third (distant), believing you can bring value that speaks to that change [1].

**Hiring is the strategy, not a task.** "There's a period at which you can be the best founder on the planet and you will be holding your own company back if you haven't figured out how to truly prioritize hiring, how to truly invest in it, and how to do it damn well." Her framing: "A founder's number one job is recruiting" [1].

**Culture before diversity.** An inclusive culture must precede diverse hiring. "What's the point of hiring all this quote diverse talent if when they get there they're not ready to succeed?" The biggest roadblock is founders thinking they need to have demographic diversity before they can work on cultural inclusion [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Nahm brings a designer's lens to company-building. She reframed her loss of product time as a CEO by recognizing "I still work on a product, the product is this company. I still have to do user research. It's just that my users are my employees and my customers" [1].

Her specific contributions to hiring practice -- motivation fit as the first interview stage, impact descriptions replacing job descriptions, proactive sourcing (81% of Lever's engineering team), nurture pipelines with two-to-three-year lead times -- are operational and concrete, grounded in Lever's own data (35% faster hire times for nurtured candidates) [1].

Holly Liu, in the interview, observes that Nahm's insight about "designers should be founders" reflects a broader theme: the skill set of understanding user needs, generating strategy around them, and building systems is deeply applicable to company-building [1].

## Key Frameworks

- **Motivation Fit**: First stage of hiring process. Ask where people are taking their career, what decisions have been meaningful, what they want more of. Listen for ambiguity comfort and specific self-awareness [1].
- **Impact Descriptions**: Replace job descriptions with 1/3/6/12-month outcome descriptions. Attracts top talent and diverse candidates by not broadcasting assumptions about who should apply [1].
- **Career Trajectory Interview**: Walk through a candidate's career from college onward, looking for patterns of success and failure. Modified from top grading methodology [1].
- **The Nurture Pipeline**: Invest in relationships years before a hire. 81% proactive sourcing rate, 35% faster time-to-hire for nurtured candidates [1].
- **Cultural Surface Area**: Culture extends to dish duty, holiday schedules, how differences are discussed. Founders must decide what is and is not part of the culture, and that boundary will shift over time [1].
