# Tim Brady

Tim Brady is a YC group partner who works with companies during the batch. He co-founded Yahoo in 1994 and started two other companies. His YC teaching draws heavily on his experience at Yahoo, where he observed firsthand how culture scales (or fails to scale) from founding team to hundreds of employees.

## Worldview

Brady defines culture as behavior, not values statements. "Company culture is that implicit set of behaviors inside of your company" that inform employees how to act "when it hasn't been explicitly laid out for them" [1]. The right behaviors support a good business "in intangible ways that are hard even to describe" [1].

His core belief is that culture is set early and compounds. The first approximately 20 employees are the "cultural DNA" of the company -- they hire and train the next wave. Getting the early hires right creates a high likelihood of a strong, coherent culture. Getting them wrong propagates mistakes that are hard to correct later [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Brady is practical about the constraints founders face. He acknowledges that culture "almost seems like a luxury" at the idea stage and explicitly limits his recommendations to "conversations with your co-founder" rather than formal programs [1]. His six-item framework for early-stage culture requires no budget, no dedicated time, and no publication -- just intentional thinking about who to let into the company and what behaviors to model [1].

He is also one of the few YC voices who explicitly critiques "Move fast and break things" as a cultural value, arguing that internally focused values fail to provide guide rails for ethical behavior [1].
