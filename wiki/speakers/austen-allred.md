# Austen Allred

Austen Allred is the CEO and co-founder of [Lambda School](https://lambdaschool.com/) (YC S17), later renamed Bloom Institute of Technology. He is not a college graduate. Before Lambda School, he founded a previous startup where a Series A fell through on December 23rd with documents already signed, leaving him broke in rural Utah with a newborn daughter in the hospital. He rebuilt by writing a growth book that generated $120,000 on Kickstarter from a list of a few thousand subscribers [1].

## Worldview

Allred views human capital as the most mispriced and unoptimized asset class in the world. His core argument: every commodity -- pork bellies, stocks, real estate -- has transparent pricing and efficient markets, but human potential has no equivalent. A person in rural Utah making $30,000 may be as capable as someone making $150,000 in San Francisco. The gap is access to opportunity, not ability [1].

He believes traditional education is structurally broken because incentives are misaligned. The University of Phoenix gets paid regardless of student outcomes. Lambda School gets paid only when students get hired, aligning the school's incentives with the student's success [1].

On product quality: Allred argues that product quality is systematically undervalued because it cannot be quantified. "Revenue is a very, very trailing indicator of product." He points to Airbnb, Stripe, Dropbox, and Tesla as companies that won through accumulated product superiority despite competitors with more resources [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Allred is unusual among YC founders for building a company that is both a technology business and an educational institution. He approaches education with the rigor of a fintech company -- underwriting student risk, building predictive models, and treating the ISA as a financial instrument. His phrase "nobody knows how to underwrite student risk" frames education as an unsolved fintech problem [1].

He is also notable for arguing that online education is superior to in-person when built from first principles, rather than being a degraded version of classroom instruction [1].

## Key Frameworks

**The ISA as Financial Innovation**: Deferred payment eliminates the biggest barrier to education (financial risk) while aligning school and student incentives [1].

**Product Quality as Compounding Advantage**: Product quality is hard to measure, which means analysts and competitors systematically underestimate it. Companies that accumulate "a whole lot of subtlety built up over time" develop durable, unmeasurable moats [1].

**Remote as a Core Innovation**: Building a great remote organization requires treating remote as one of the company's core innovations, not an add-on. It consumes innovation budget but unlocks talent advantages [1].
