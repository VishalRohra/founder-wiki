# Gustaf Alströmer

Gustaf Alströmer is a Y Combinator group partner (since 2017), former Head of Growth at Airbnb, and a YC founder from the 2007 batch. He provides the most systematic treatment of user research and early-stage sales in the YC canon.

## Worldview

Alströmer's advice is grounded in direct user contact as the foundation of everything. His recurring themes:

**Users keep you honest.** Users and customers are the only stakeholders actually paying you anything. If anyone will tell you the truth, it will be them [1]. Great founders talk to future customers before they even have a product, and the very best founders learn directly from users throughout the lifetime of their company [1].

**Sales is a numbers game.** Most founders do not send enough outreach because they do not work backwards from their goals [2]. Sending 100 emails and getting zero customers is not evidence that sales does not work -- it is evidence of insufficient volume. Successful startups internalize this [2].

**Focus on problems, not features.** The biggest danger during user interviews is focusing on features instead of problems. Users generally have good problems but bad solutions [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Alströmer bridges growth strategy at scale (his [[Growth]] team guide synthesizes advice from 25 growth experts [3]) with ground-level tactical advice for pre-revenue startups. Where other partners give philosophical advice, Alströmer provides specific email templates, conversion funnel math, and CRM tool recommendations [2].

His background at Airbnb gives him firsthand knowledge of how doing things that don't scale transitions into scalable growth. He emphasizes that Airbnb's eventual growth channels (word of mouth, Google search, referrals, Facebook advertising) are not how they got their first customers. They did things that did not scale [2].

## Key Frameworks

- **The Six Interview Questions**: A structured framework for user interviews: how do you do X today, what is hardest, why is it hard, how often, why is it important, what do you currently do to solve it [1]
- **The Sales Funnel Math**: Working backwards from goals through conversion rates (outreach -> open -> response -> demo -> close -> onboard) [2]
- **Easiest Customers First**: Sell to people you know, then startups, then larger companies. Most people are not early adopters [2]
- **Retention Benchmarks by Vertical**: Specific long-term retention targets for social networks (45-65%), on-demand (20-30%), travel (20-35%), e-commerce (10-25%), and subscription (25-35%) [3]
