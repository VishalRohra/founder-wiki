# Jared Friedman

Jared Friedman is a partner at Y Combinator and co-founder of Scribd. He leads YC's work on hard tech and biotech companies and has built much of YC's internal AI tooling.

## Worldview

**Hard tech and biotech need founder-friendly infrastructure.** Friedman's work on university spinouts and biotech funding reflects a belief that the startup model -- speed, founder control, iterative development -- can work in domains traditionally dominated by slow-moving academic and corporate research [1] [2].

**Prompting is the new coding.** Through building internal AI tools at YC, Friedman has developed practical techniques for production prompt engineering, including the "debug info" pattern where the LLM reports what is confusing about its instructions -- creating a to-do list for the developer [3].

**Being early looks like being wrong.** Friedman's observation about the 2019 MLOps wave -- more YC applications for ML tooling than for companies using ML -- captures a pattern he has seen repeatedly. Companies like Replicate, Ollama, and Deepgram built in obscurity for years before their markets materialized. The lesson: following genuine curiosity about technology is the best predictor of being in the right place when the market arrives [4].

## Key Frameworks

- **The debug info pattern**: Give the LLM an output parameter for complaining about underspecified instructions, then use those complaints as a development to-do list [3].
- **Follow curiosity, not market timing**: The companies that were positioned for the AI wave followed genuine interest in ML, not market analysis [4].
- **Fork and merge prompts**: The emerging pattern of separating system prompts (company-wide) from developer prompts (customer-specific) to scale without becoming a consulting company [3].
