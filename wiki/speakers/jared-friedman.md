# Jared Friedman

Jared Friedman is a partner at Y Combinator who specializes in advising scientific and hard tech founders. He has worked with 75+ YC companies spinning out of universities and conducted YC's Bio Tour visiting research universities to talk with hundreds of students and professors about commercializing research [1].

## Worldview

Friedman is blunt about the misconceptions that hold scientific founders back. His core message: scientists overvalue business experience and undervalue their own ability to run a company. They delay too long, defer to outside CEOs they do not need, and misallocate equity by rewarding past contributions rather than anticipating future ones [1].

He is particularly pointed about the catch-22 that traps academic founders: they wait for investor validation before committing, while investors wait for founder commitment before investing [1].

## Key Frameworks

**The Marathon Analogy**: At spinout, founders feel halfway done. They are on mile 2 of 26. Academic founders were instrumental in mile 1; full-time founders take the other 25. Equity must reflect the full marathon, not the first mile [1].

**Four Misconceptions of Scientific Founders**: (1) You can run a company from academia, (2) you should find an outside CEO, (3) you need business experience, (4) you should raise money before leaving. All four are wrong [1].

**Tech Transfer Terms**: Four key terms in university license agreements -- equity (3-5% typical), royalty (ideally zero), milestone payments, and sublicensing terms [1].
