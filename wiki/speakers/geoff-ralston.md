# Geoff Ralston

Geoff Ralston is a YC partner and the author of YC's comprehensive guide to seed fundraising. He draws on experience working at startups, investing in startups, and advising startups at Y Combinator and Imagine K12.

## Worldview

Ralston's approach to fundraising is practical and process-oriented. He provides specific numbers (engineer costs ~$15K/month, seed rounds cluster around $600K, valuations range $2M-$10M) and concrete do's and don'ts. His overarching message: raise quickly and get back to work.

He emphasizes that fundraising is a necessary evil, not a milestone: "once you turn your attention back to the future you will realize it was only a small foothill on the real climb in front of you."

## Key Contributions

- **Seed Fundraising Guide**: Comprehensive coverage of when to raise, how much, financing instruments (SAFEs, convertible debt, equity), types of investors, meeting investors, and negotiation
- **Fundraising rules**: Be greedy (breadth-first search weighted by expected value), get the first money in (hardest part), close quickly, never screw anyone over
- **Startup Priorities**: Referenced in YC's essential advice for choosing what to work on

## Distinct Perspective

Ralston is unusually specific about the mechanics of fundraising. He provides example math for equity rounds (showing that $1M on a $5M pre-money valuation yields 16.7% dilution, not 20%), explains the difference between angels and VCs in behavioral terms, and lists specific platforms (Clerky, Ironclad) for closing.
