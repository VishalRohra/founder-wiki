# Brad Flora

Brad Flora is a Y Combinator group partner who has seen startup fundraising from every angle: as founder of Perfect Audience (YC S11, acquired 2014), as one of Silicon Valley's most prolific angel investors (150 YC company investments through a fund, including Retool, Razorpay, and OpenSea), and as a YC partner [1]. He is a core participant in the "Group Partner Lounge" video series alongside [[Michael Seibel]] and [[Harj Taggar]].

## Worldview

Flora's advice centers on demystifying processes that founders over-complicate. His recurring themes:

**Fundraising is a grind, not a spectacle.** Actual fundraising looks like a bunch of coffee chats and Zoom calls, not Shark Tank [1]. Flora dispels myths systematically: you do not need money before starting, your startup does not need to be impressive (it needs to convince, not impress), and SAFEs have made early fundraising fast, cheap, and founder-friendly [1].

**Build first, explain simply.** Retool's founder David had no deck -- he just opened his laptop, showed the software, made a crude internal tool in minutes, and talked about why early customers liked it. That was enough to raise at what became a $4B valuation [1].

**Believe the no, not the why.** When investors reject you, accept the rejection but do not internalize their stated reasons. Investors are not experts on your business, and the stated reason is rarely the real one [3]. The way to change an investor's mind is not with words but with actions -- make progress, and they will come back [3].

## Key Frameworks

- **Seven Fundraising Myths**: Fundraising is glamorous (no, it is coffee chats), you need money before starting (no, build first), your startup needs to be impressive (no, convince not impress), fundraising is complicated (no, SAFEs), you'll lose control (no, SAFEs give no board seats), you need a fancy network (no, make something people want), rejection means bad startup (no, even $3.7B WhatNot could barely raise at seed) [1]
- **The KPI Test for Pivoting**: If you do not have a main KPI you are obsessively tracking, you may not realize your business is not working. Flora discovered this when Creative Market's marketplace revenue gave immediate clarity that Color Lovers' community never did [5]
- **The Civilization Fallacy**: Founders who want to treat their startup like a real-time strategy game, clicking on things from a computer and watching numbers go up, rather than doing actual sales [6]
