# Harj Taggar

Harj Taggar is cofounder of [Triplebyte](https://triplebyte.com/) (YC S15), a hiring platform that helps engineers find work at fast-growing companies. His perspective on hiring is informed by both sides of the marketplace: he observes how companies hire (with acceptance rates ranging from 10% to 90%) and how engineers evaluate offers.

## Worldview

Taggar treats hiring as an optimization problem, similar to product development. He provides specific data points (industry standard 20% onsite-to-offer rate, 30 hours of engineering productivity per offer generated) and argues that improving acceptance rates provides direct economic value. His two posts form a complete hiring funnel: finding candidates, then convincing them to accept.

His core insight: personal networks are the dominant channel for early hiring, but most founders do not fully explore them because "asking your friends to leave their jobs and take a risk with you is scary." He ranks all sourcing channels from most to least effective and provides specific processes for each.

## Key Frameworks

- **Sourcing channel ranking**: Personal networks > hiring marketplaces > inbound > cold outreach > recruiters > meetups > agencies
- **Speed as competitive advantage**: Startups' main edge over large companies in hiring
- **Expiring signing bonus**: A softer alternative to exploding offers that maintains closing pressure without the cultural baggage
- **Arguments against big companies**: Learning, career progression, opportunity cost, mentorship

## Distinct Perspective

Taggar is unusually data-driven about hiring. He cites specific conversion rates and provides detailed agenda structures for calls and onsites. He emphasizes the candidate experience at every touchpoint and argues that bad interviewer behavior (being unprepared, unfamiliar with questions, or trying to prove intelligence) is the primary cause of lost candidates.
