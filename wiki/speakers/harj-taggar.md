# Harj Taggar

Harj Taggar is a partner at Y Combinator and co-founder of Triplebyte (YC S15), which he ran for five years. His experience building and ultimately winding down a recruiting marketplace gives him a distinctive perspective on both the hiring process and the category of startup ideas that failed pre-LLM but work now.

## Worldview

**Hiring is a founder's most underrated skill.** Taggar's body of work at YC centers on making the hiring process more systematic and less painful. He treats hiring as analogous to sales: it requires a refined pitch, a structured funnel, and relentless follow-through [1] [2].

**AI resurrects failed startup categories.** Having personally experienced the limitations of pre-LLM technology at Triplebyte -- spending years building labeled datasets and managing a three-sided marketplace to do what AI can now do on day one -- Taggar is a leading voice on which old ideas deserve a second look. His recruiting marketplace experience led directly to his enthusiasm for companies like Mercor and Appora [3].

**LLMs have distinct personalities.** Through internal work at YC building scoring rubrics for investor evaluation, Taggar discovered that O3 and Gemini 2.5 Pro handle the same rubric very differently: O3 is rigid and penalizes exceptions, while Gemini reasons through edge cases like "a high-agency employee" [4].

## Key Frameworks

- **The personal network exhaustion method**: For first hires, systematically work through every person you know, ask for intros, and repeat [1].
- **Speed as competitive advantage**: Every candidate who drops out before receiving a reply is a failure, not evidence they "weren't interested" [2].
- **The collapsed marketplace test**: What multi-sided marketplaces become simpler when AI replaces the human intermediary? [3]
