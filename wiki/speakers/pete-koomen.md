# Pete Koomen

Pete Koomen is a YC Group Partner and co-founder of Optimizely (YC W10), which he scaled to $100M ARR. His background is technical (CTO), making his perspective on sales particularly valuable: he learned to sell as an engineer and argues that technical founders are often better at sales than they expect.

## Worldview

Koomen believes sales is a learnable skill, not a dark art. "Sales isn't about tricking people. It's fundamentally about helping people solve their problems, and engineers are great at doing that" [1]. His central argument: founders must sell before hiring salespeople, because pre-PMF sales requires vision, credibility, and tight product feedback loops that only founders can provide [1].

## Key Frameworks

**The Founder-Led Sales Funnel**: Prospect, outreach, qualify, demo, price, close, implement -- each step has specific founder actions. The most common mistake at every stage is not asking enough questions [1].

**Demo as Story**: Great demos are not feature tours. They are structured like movies: introduce the main character (the user), establish the problem, then show the resolution. Personalize with the customer's logo, website, and team names [1].

**Implementation as Sales**: "The single biggest mistake founders make is thinking implementation is the customer's job." Optimizely lost six-figure customers who never ran a single A/B test because marketing could not convince engineering to install the code [1].

**Conversion Design**: From his design review work, Koomen emphasizes reducing friction to the "aha moment." Every step between landing on a website and experiencing value should be scrutinized for removal [2].

## Distinct Perspective

As a technical co-founder who became a skilled seller, Koomen directly contradicts the assumption that engineers cannot sell. He provides the most detailed step-by-step sales playbook in the YC canon, with specific email templates, question frameworks, and pricing tactics drawn from Optimizely's growth.
