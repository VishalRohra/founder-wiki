# Kevin Hale

Kevin Hale is a YC Group Partner and co-founder of Wufoo, the form-builder acquired by SurveyMonkey. He is known for his talk on pitch deck design, which introduced the [[Legible Simple Obvious]] framework.

## Worldview

Hale approaches startup communication as a design problem. His central argument is that most pitch decks fail not because the underlying business is bad, but because the presentation is poorly designed. The same principles that make products easy to use -- clarity, simplicity, and obviousness -- apply to investor pitches [1].

He draws on his experience at Wufoo, where the company became known for obsessive attention to user experience and for practices like sending hand-written thank-you notes to users. Hale carries this same attention to craft into how he teaches founders to communicate.

## On API Companies and Vertical Integration

In his interview with [[Harry Zhang]], Hale contributed substantive observations about API companies and vertical integration.

**API documentation as interface design**: Hale argues that API companies represent the purest form of interface design. "A lot of people think of it as 'Oh, it has no sort of interface.' But to me, the interface is just documentation. It's very pure" [2]. He evaluates API companies by how thoughtful their documentation is, treating it as a signal of engineering quality and community-building intent. Stripe is his reference standard [2].

**Vertical integration question**: Hale pressed Zhang on whether Lob would eventually buy printing facilities, drawing the Amazon analogy -- Amazon could have continued using third-party logistics but eventually built its own warehouses for efficiency and vision. He frames the decision point: vertical integration makes sense when "you have a vision for the future that requires you to own that technology set" [2].

**Non-sexy businesses that make money**: Hale expresses his preference for unglamorous businesses: "I love these non-sexy businesses, especially ones that make money" [2].

## Key Frameworks

**[[Legible Simple Obvious]]**: Hale's framework for evaluating any piece of communication, particularly pitch decks. Legible means the audience can physically read and parse the information. Simple means the core message is not buried under complexity. Obvious means the conclusion the presenter wants the audience to reach is unmistakable [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Hale is distinctive for treating the pitch deck as a product design challenge rather than a sales exercise. Where other pitch advisors focus on narrative and persuasion, Hale focuses on information design -- font sizes, data density, visual hierarchy, and cognitive load. As an interviewer, he probes founders on strategic decisions about vertical integration and infrastructure ownership.
