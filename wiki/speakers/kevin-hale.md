# Kevin Hale

Kevin Hale is a YC partner known for his guidance on pitch deck design [1]. His framework is used with every YC batch before Demo Day. [[Aaron Harris]] references Hale's design piece as the complement to his seed deck content template.

## Worldview

Hale approaches deck design from a communication theory perspective, not a graphic design one [1]. His core insight: "Investors invest in teams not slides. Your slides should make your ideas more clear" [1]. The goal is understanding, not beauty. The three principles (legible, simple, obvious) are derived from how human cognition works, not from aesthetic preferences [1].

He cites [Hick's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hick%27s_law) to explain why complexity hurts: each piece of information added to a slide increases comprehension time logarithmically [1]. He calls diagrams "little mazes for ideas" [1].

## Key Framework

**[[Pitch Deck Design|Legible, Simple, Obvious]]**: The complete framework for pitch deck design [1].

- **Legible**: Large type, bold, simple font, good contrast, text at top. Designed for "old people sitting in the back row with bad eyesight" [1].
- **Simple**: One idea per slide. The word "plex" (as in com-plex) means braid/fold. Simple = one fold = one idea. 5-7 slides for 5-7 ideas [1].
- **Obvious**: Understood at a glance. Test with a stranger. Make ideas explicit (caption growth graphs). Avoid information distractions [1].

The one exception: deliberately show complexity/overwhelm when the point is to illustrate the scale of a problem [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Hale is strongly opinionated about screenshots: "Screenshots are almost always illegible, complex, and non-obvious. They break all 3 rules!" [1]. He recommends bulleted step lists instead of UI screenshots to explain products [1].
