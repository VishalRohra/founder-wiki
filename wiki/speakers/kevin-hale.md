# Kevin Hale

Kevin Hale is a YC partner known for his guidance on pitch deck design. His framework is used with every YC batch before Demo Day. [[Aaron Harris]] references Hale's design piece as the complement to his seed deck content template.

## Worldview

Hale approaches deck design from a communication theory perspective, not a graphic design one. His core insight: "Investors invest in teams not slides. Your slides should make your ideas more clear." The goal is understanding, not beauty. The three principles (legible, simple, obvious) are derived from how human cognition works, not from aesthetic preferences.

He cites [Hick's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hick%27s_law) to explain why complexity hurts: each piece of information added to a slide increases comprehension time logarithmically. He calls diagrams "little mazes for ideas."

## Key Framework

**Legible, Simple, Obvious**: The complete framework for pitch deck design.

- **Legible**: Large type, bold, simple font, good contrast, text at top. Designed for "old people sitting in the back row with bad eyesight."
- **Simple**: One idea per slide. The word "plex" (as in com-plex) means braid/fold. Simple = one fold = one idea. 5-7 slides for 5-7 ideas.
- **Obvious**: Understood at a glance. Test with a stranger. Make ideas explicit (caption growth graphs). Avoid information distractions.

The one exception: deliberately show complexity/overwhelm when the point is to illustrate the scale of a problem.

## Distinct Perspective

Hale is strongly opinionated about screenshots: "Screenshots are almost always illegible, complex, and non-obvious. They break all 3 rules!" He recommends bulleted step lists instead of UI screenshots to explain products.
