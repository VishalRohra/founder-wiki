# Janelle Tam

Janelle Tam is YC's Series A Program Manager. She spent hundreds of hours working 1:1 with founders to workshop their [[Series A Pitch and Deck|Series A pitches]] and decks [1].

## Worldview

Tam's feedback is highly tactical and presentation-focused [1]:

- **Fundraising vertebrae.** Distill your pitch to 10-15 bullet points. Each becomes one slide. Each slide makes one point. "If you're trying to make more than one point per slide, your slide is too complicated" [1].
- **Show, not tell.** Quantify everything. Side-by-side comparisons of before/after your product. "Don't tell me you have the fastest/easiest/cheapest product, actually show me numbers" [1].
- **Clarity over beauty.** Deck design should aid comprehension, not impress aesthetically [1]. Avoid decorative images and complex diagrams. See also [[Pitch Deck Design]] for [[Kevin Hale]]'s complementary framework.
- **Traction storytelling.** Numbers should tell a story starting with the most important metric (usually revenue) [1]. Show monthly trends, not cumulative numbers. "Almost every time I've seen cumulative numbers in a Series A deck, it's because founders are trying to hide their monthly/quarterly numbers" [1].
- **The ask is the climax.** Many decks omit the use-of-funds slide [1]. It should show how much you need, what you will do with it, and where it gets you in 18-24 months.
