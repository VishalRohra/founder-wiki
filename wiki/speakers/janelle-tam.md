# Janelle Tam

Janelle Tam is YC's Series A Program Manager. She spent hundreds of hours working 1:1 with founders to workshop their Series A pitches and decks.

## Worldview

Tam's feedback is highly tactical and presentation-focused:

- **Fundraising vertebrae.** Distill your pitch to 10-15 bullet points. Each becomes one slide. Each slide makes one point. "If you're trying to make more than one point per slide, your slide is too complicated."
- **Show, not tell.** Quantify everything. Side-by-side comparisons of before/after your product. "Don't tell me you have the fastest/easiest/cheapest product, actually show me numbers."
- **Clarity over beauty.** Deck design should aid comprehension, not impress aesthetically. Avoid decorative images and complex diagrams.
- **Traction storytelling.** Numbers should tell a story starting with the most important metric (usually revenue). Show monthly trends, not cumulative numbers. "Almost every time I've seen cumulative numbers in a Series A deck, it's because founders are trying to hide their monthly/quarterly numbers."
- **The ask is the climax.** Many decks omit the use-of-funds slide. It should show how much you need, what you will do with it, and where it gets you in 18-24 months.
