# Aaron Epstein

Aaron Epstein is a YC Group Partner and founder of Creative Market, a marketplace for graphic design assets. He went through YC in 2010. His advice covers the full range of early-stage commercialization: [[Business Models]], [[Cold Emails]], and [[Pricing and Monetization]] [1][2].

## Worldview

**Copy the business model, innovate the product.** Epstein analyzed the YC Top 100 and found that nearly every billion-dollar company uses one of nine proven business models. Founders who try to invent a new way to make money are solving the wrong problem [1].

**Get as close to the transaction as possible.** Advice he received during his own YC batch in 2010. Companies that literally process money (Stripe, Brex) become critical infrastructure. Companies far from the transaction (affiliates) struggle [1].

**Cold outreach is a system, not an art.** Epstein treats email outreach as a measurable funnel with known conversion rates. Founders who expect results from a handful of emails per week are under-investing. The math says: plan for hundreds of emails to get one customer [2].

## Distinct Perspective

Epstein combines quantitative frameworks (funnel math, business model categorization) with granular tactical advice (subject line examples, read-it-aloud tests, before/after email rewrites). He is unusually willing to show real emails he received and critique them publicly. His before-and-after rewrite of a smoothie company's outreach email demonstrates how reframing from the sender's needs to the recipient's benefit transforms response rates [2].

## Key Frameworks

- **The Nine Business Models**: SaaS, transactional, marketplace, subscription, enterprise, usage-based, e-commerce, advertising, hard tech/bio [1]
- **The Cold Email Funnel**: Sent → Opened → Responded → Demo → Customer, with quantified conversion rates at each step [2]
- **Seven Principles of Effective Email Copy**: Focused goal, be human, personalize, keep it short, establish credibility, make it about the reader, clear call to action [2]
- **Uncommon Commonalities**: Find things uniquely in common with the recipient that signal genuine research and connection [2]
