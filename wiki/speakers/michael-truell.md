# Michael Truell

Michael Truell is co-founder and CEO of Anysphere, the company behind Cursor, one of the fastest-growing AI coding tools. He co-founded the company at age 23.

## Worldview

**Taste is the enduring human skill.** In a world where code generation is cheap, the differentiator is knowing what good software feels like. Truell hired Cursor's first 10 employees for their aesthetic judgment about user experience as much as their technical ability [1].

**The goal is beyond code.** Cursor's long-term vision is a world where programming languages are an intermediate representation, not the primary interface. The human specifies intent; the machine handles implementation. Truell sees programming as a transitional technology -- powerful but destined to be abstracted away [1].

**Years of false starts are normal.** Cursor's success followed years of wrong directions: the team initially worked on AI-powered CAD tools before pivoting to code. Multiple rewrites and product directions preceded the breakthrough. Truell does not frame this as wasted time but as essential learning about what AI tools needed to be [1].

## Key Frameworks

- **Taste-first hiring**: Select for aesthetic judgment and product intuition, not just coding ability. The first 10 hires set the culture [1].
- **Pivoting toward leverage**: When choosing between multiple promising directions, choose the one where AI capability creates the most leverage over the status quo [1].

## Advice to Engineering Students

In a separate interview, Truell advises engineering students to focus on building things that people actually use, rather than optimizing for academic metrics. The experience of shipping a product to real users and watching how they interact with it teaches more about product development than any course [2].
