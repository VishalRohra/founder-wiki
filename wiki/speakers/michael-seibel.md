# Michael Seibel

Michael Seibel is a YC Group Partner and former CEO of Y Combinator. Before YC, he cofounded Justin.tv (2007) and Socialcam. Justin.tv eventually pivoted into [[Twitch]], which was acquired by Amazon. Socialcam was acquired by Autodesk.

## Worldview

Seibel's advice is grounded in personal experience building consumer products through rapid iteration. He is a strong advocate for:

- **Structured product development.** At Socialcam, he developed a two-week development cycle with organized brainstorms, consensus-driven prioritization, mandatory analytics specs, and no roadmap beyond the current cycle. He learned from years of dysfunction at Justin.tv, where development cycles ran months and "product decisions were made exclusively by the founders in a non-transparent process."

- **PR as business development.** After spending over $100,000 on PR agencies at his early-stage startup, Seibel learned from Mike Arrington and M.G. Siegler to treat PR like biz dev: build direct reporter relationships through warm intros, offer exclusives, and maintain a consistent cadence of real news. "Make sure you aren't dedicating more than 5% of your time to press."

- **Urgency for young people.** Seibel repeatedly emphasizes that ages 22-32 are the best startup years. "Every year you are not doing a startup you are losing 10% of this prime time." He advises students to learn to code, meet skilled people, avoid debt, and be their own career counselors.

- **No value judgment between career paths.** Seibel lays out founder, executive, and employee as three legitimate tech career paths, each with distinct strategies and tradeoffs. He is explicit that experience is "massively over-valued" for aspiring founders.

## Key Frameworks

- **Two-week development cycle** with easy/medium/hard grading, consensus prioritization, and mandatory analytics (see [[Product Development]])
- **Three product meeting goals**: increasing content creation, increasing new users, increasing retention
- **PR cadence**: maintain a queue of 3-5 announcements over 6 months (see [[Press and PR]])
- **Four founder prerequisites**: teammates, financial plan, passionate problem, idea (in that order)
