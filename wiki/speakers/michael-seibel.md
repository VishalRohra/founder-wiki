# Michael Seibel

Michael Seibel is a YC Group Partner and former CEO of Y Combinator. Before YC, he cofounded [[Justin.tv]] (2007) and Socialcam. Justin.tv eventually pivoted into [[Twitch]], which was acquired by Amazon. Socialcam was acquired by Autodesk.

## Worldview

Seibel's advice is grounded in personal experience building consumer products through rapid iteration. He is a strong advocate for:

- **Structured [[Product Development|product development]].** At Socialcam, he developed a two-week development cycle with organized brainstorms, consensus-driven prioritization, mandatory analytics specs, and no roadmap beyond the current cycle [3]. He learned from years of dysfunction at Justin.tv, where development cycles ran months and "product decisions were made exclusively by the founders in a non-transparent process" [3].

- **[[Press and PR|PR as business development]].** After spending over $100,000 on PR agencies at his early-stage startup, Seibel learned from Mike Arrington and M.G. Siegler to treat PR like biz dev: build direct reporter relationships through warm intros, offer exclusives, and maintain a consistent cadence of real news [1]. "Make sure you aren't dedicating more than 5% of your time to press" [1].

- **Urgency for young people.** Seibel repeatedly emphasizes that ages 22-32 are the best startup years [2]. "Every year you are not doing a startup you are losing 10% of this prime time." He advises students to learn to code, meet skilled people, avoid debt, and be their own career counselors [2].

- **No value judgment between career paths.** Seibel lays out founder, executive, and employee as three legitimate tech career paths, each with distinct strategies and tradeoffs [4]. He is explicit that experience is "massively over-valued" for aspiring founders [4].

## Key Frameworks

- **Two-week development cycle** with easy/medium/hard grading, consensus prioritization, and mandatory analytics (see [[Product Development]]) [3]
- **Three product meeting goals**: increasing content creation, increasing new users, increasing retention [3]
- **PR cadence**: maintain a queue of 3-5 announcements over 6 months (see [[Press and PR]]) [1]
- **Four founder prerequisites**: teammates, financial plan, passionate problem, idea (in that order) [2]
