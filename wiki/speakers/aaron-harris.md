# Aaron Harris

Aaron Harris is a YC partner who focuses on fundraising mechanics: pitch decks, term sheets, and diligence processes. He also writes at [aaronkharris.com](http://www.aaronkharris.com/advice-on-pitching).

## Worldview

Harris's approach to fundraising emphasizes clarity and practicality. His seed deck template is deliberately simple, built on the principle that "there isn't very much meaningful detail to explore for most seed stage companies. When founders pretend that there is, their stories get muddled, and the investors get lost."

On term sheets, Harris (with [[Jason Kwon]]) focuses on helping founders understand what "good" looks like, because first-time founders are at a structural disadvantage: "VCs see term sheets all the time and know what to expect." His most pointed advice: when investor words and terms disagree, "believe the terms."

## Key Contributions

- **YC Seed Deck Template**: Title, problem, solution, traction, insights, business model, market, team, the ask. "Focus on narrative. The rest is commentary."
- **Standard Series A Term Sheet**: A clean, one-page template showing what standard terms look like. Highlights dirty terms to watch for (>1x liquidation preference, participating preferred, cumulative dividends, warrant coverage).
- **Series A Diligence Checklist**: Comprehensive list of documents needed for a data room, covering corporate records, IP, security issuances, material agreements, litigation, and employee information. Having it ready "will cut as much as a week off of your closing process."
- **Equity grants warning**: Complete all pending equity grants before starting Series A, because a term sheet may invalidate the current 409A valuation.
