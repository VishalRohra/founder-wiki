# Kat Manalac

Kat Manalac is a Partner at Y Combinator. Before YC, she worked at Wired magazine. At YC, she helps founders prepare to launch and is one of the primary voices on launch strategy. She appears across two major sources in this wiki.

## Worldview

Manalac's core belief is that launching is continuous, not singular. She works to destroy what she sees as the most damaging misconception in early-stage startups: that a launch is a one-shot, high-production event [1].

**Launch is not a moment -- it is a process.** "I want to destroy the idea that launching is just this one moment in time because assuming you do well, and assuming you ship new products and new features, you are never going to be not launching" [1].

**Pre-product launches are valuable.** Even before a functioning product, founders can test their pitch, gauge audience interest, and build a community. Manalac catalogs multiple launch types (silent, friends and family, strangers, online communities, waitlists, blogger outreach, pre-order) that can be executed at any stage [1][2].

**Write like a human.** Manalac repeatedly emphasizes: "Do not talk like a marketing robot. People hate that" [1]. Community posts should introduce the founder, explain the product, share why it matters, and invite questions -- in plain language [1].

**Build your community before you need it.** Manalac highlights the Cymmetria example: the founder built an email list over years by adding every person he discussed his startup with, then activated that list when their TechCrunch article launched [1].

## Distinct Perspective

Manalac is the most comprehensive cataloger of launch types in the YC canon. Where [[Michael Seibel]] and [[Harj Taggar]] argue philosophically for launching early, Manalac provides the practical playbook: here are the seven types of launches, here is how each one works, here are real examples.

She also brings the perspective of someone who worked in media (Wired) before joining YC, which gives her credibility when advising against over-reliance on press. She is the one who explicitly sets press aside for early-stage companies: it is not scalable, not a silver bullet, and not the path to product-market fit [2].

## Key Frameworks

- **Continuous Launching**: Launching is not one event. Every new feature, product, or community is a launch opportunity [1][2].
- **Seven Launch Types**: Silent, friends and family, strangers, online communities, waitlist/request for access, blogger/SEO, and new feature launches [1][2].
- **Community-First Distribution**: Build an email list or community before needing it, then activate when you have something to share [1].
