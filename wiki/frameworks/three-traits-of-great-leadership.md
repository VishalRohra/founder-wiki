# Three Traits of Great Leadership

The Three Traits of Great Leadership is a measurement framework developed by [[Ali Rowghani]] based on his experience working closely with Ed Catmull, Steve Jobs, John Lasseter, and Bob Iger at Pixar and Disney [1]. Despite these four leaders being radically different in style and temperament, Rowghani found they all built extraordinary trust through the same three traits.

The framework's core claim: "You cannot be a great leader without [these three traits] because you cannot build trust without them" [1].

## The Three Traits

### 1. Clarity of Thought and Communication

Great leaders think clearly and describe a compelling vision [1]. If employees are "confused about your mission and strategy, or do not find it motivating or credible, they will not follow you with the focus and determination necessary to succeed" [1].

**How to improve**: Spend more time thinking about what is truly important, then practice expressing it simply [1]. Clarity of thought always precedes clarity of language.

**Exemplar**: Jeff Bezos's retail strategy at Amazon, built on three enduring customer preferences: lower prices, bigger selection, faster delivery. As Bezos said: "You can build a business strategy around the things that are stable in time" [1].

**At scale**: Great leaders spend hours preparing internal communications, regardless of natural talent. Tobi Lütke of Shopify spends hundreds of hours preparing for the annual employee Summit because "if we communicate well at the Summit, we achieve great alignment by the end" [1].

### 2. Judgment About People

Great leaders have strong intuition about who to empower. They "see hidden potential in people and detect cases where ambition exceeds ability" [1]. They also have the courage to correct hiring and promotion mistakes.

**The stakes**: "Nothing does more damage to an organization or to the standing of a leader than picking the wrong leaders or failing to correct these mistakes" [1]. But "leaders who fire too many of their own also lose a lot of credibility and trust" [1].

**How to improve**: Meet the best people in every field to sharpen pattern recognition. Do extensive reference checks. Learn from mistakes [1].

### 3. Personal Integrity and Commitment

Integrity means "standing for something meaningful beyond oneself" [1]. It includes admitting mistakes, receiving critical feedback openly, and avoiding trust-eroding behaviors (favoritism, conflicts of interest, inappropriate relationships).

**The test**: "if your team had full transparency into your private communications and behavior towards employees, would you be embarrassed by anything you have done or said?" [1].

Commitment goes beyond time investment. Great leaders "make their work into their core life mission in ways that inspire others" [1].

## Measuring Leadership with Surveys

Rowghani proposes structured employee surveys organized around the three traits. Startups should begin this at approximately 50 employees [1]. The survey design compares CEO self-assessment with employee perception to identify alignment gaps.

### Clarity of Thought Questions

**For the CEO** [1]:
- Write down your company's mission, strategy, and key metrics in less than 2 minutes
- Write down 2-3 themes you consistently emphasize in communications

**For employees** [1]:
- What is the company's mission and strategy?
- What are the most important operating metrics?
- How does your work contribute to key success metrics?
- How often has the definition of mission, strategy, and metrics changed in the past 24 months?
- What do you think is really important to the CEO?
- How effective is the CEO in written, large group, and small group communication?

### Judgment About People Questions

**For the CEO** [1]:
- Rate the effectiveness of each leader you have promoted or hired
- Name any leader you don't think is the right person for their role
- Have you exited the right employees?

**For employees** [1]:
- Has the CEO chosen good leaders?
- Which leaders do you respect and why?
- Are there leaders you think are weak and why?
- Were any recent leadership changes good decisions?
- Have high performers left the company in the past year? Why?
- (For departing employees) Are you leaving because of concerns about senior leadership?

### Personal Integrity Questions

**For the CEO** [1]:
- Have you taken actions that may have diminished employee confidence in your integrity?
- Do you ask for performance feedback? Can you cite examples of behavior change from feedback?
- How do you rate your commitment level? Your direct reports'?

**For employees** [1]:
- How would you rate the CEO's integrity/moral compass?
- Does the CEO listen well and respond to feedback?
- Have you seen favoritism, inappropriate relationships, conflicts of interest, or unethical behavior?
- What do employees feel motivates the CEO?
- How would you describe the CEO's personal commitment to the mission?
- Have you seen examples of lack of commitment from the CEO or other leaders?

## Why Not Measure by Business Results

Rowghani rejects measuring leadership through company performance because "timing and luck can be confounding factors" and because performance metrics provide "no clues as to how someone can improve as a leader" [1]. The three-trait framework is both diagnostic (where are you strong and weak?) and actionable (each trait has specific improvement paths).

## The Trust Equation

Trust is built through both competence (the "science") and character (the "art") [1]:

- **Science of trust**: Making accurate predictions about products, investments, and competitive landscapes. Choosing the right people for leadership roles. Getting things done.
- **Art of trust**: Saying the right thing at the right time. Standing for ideals bigger than yourself. Being honest about what you don't know. Not trying to be someone else.

"This is why you can't try to copy Steve Jobs or Ed Catmull in your quest to be a great leader. You can only be yourself" [1].

The operational lesson for difficult decisions: "ask yourself which course of action and which style of communication will increase the trust that employees have in them. When faced with a difficult challenge, they optimize for trust" [1].
