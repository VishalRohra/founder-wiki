# SAFEs and Priced Rounds

SAFEs (Simple Agreements for Future Equity) and priced equity rounds are the two primary mechanisms for startup financing. Kirsty Nathoo, YC CFO, explains the mechanics, trade-offs, and common mistakes founders make with each instrument [1].

## SAFEs

A SAFE is not a loan. It is an agreement that converts into equity at a future financing event. YC introduced the SAFE to simplify seed-stage fundraising, and it has become the dominant instrument for early rounds [1].

Key terms:
- **Valuation cap**: The maximum valuation at which the SAFE converts. If the next round is priced above the cap, the SAFE holder converts at the cap (getting a better price). If the next round is below the cap, the SAFE holder converts at the round price.
- **Discount**: An alternative or addition to the cap. The SAFE holder gets a percentage discount on the next round price.
- **Post-money vs. pre-money**: YC's current SAFE template is post-money, meaning the cap includes the SAFE money itself. This makes dilution calculations clearer for founders [1].

Advantages of SAFEs:
- No interest rate or maturity date
- Minimal closing mechanics (sign and wire money)
- No board seat or control provisions
- Standardized documents reduce legal costs [1]

## Priced Rounds

A priced equity round sets a specific per-share price and issues new shares. This is standard for Series A and later rounds but rare at seed stage due to complexity and cost [1].

Key elements:
- Board composition is negotiated
- Investor rights (information rights, pro rata, anti-dilution)
- Vesting schedules are formalized
- Legal fees are significantly higher ($15K-$40K+ vs. minimal for SAFEs) [1]

## Common Mistakes

Nathoo identifies several mistakes founders make [1]:
- **Raising too many SAFEs at different caps**: Creates a confusing cap table when conversion happens
- **Not understanding dilution**: Post-money SAFEs make dilution predictable; pre-money SAFEs do not
- **Ignoring the pro rata**: Some SAFEs include pro rata rights, which can affect future round dynamics
