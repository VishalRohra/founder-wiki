# Startup Legal Mechanics

The legal setup of a startup is one of the first operational decisions founders must make. Kirsty Nathoo, YC CFO, walks through the standard setup and common mistakes in a Startup School lecture [1].

## Standard Setup: Delaware C-Corp

YC recommends incorporating as a Delaware C-Corp. The reasons [1]:
- Delaware corporate law is the most developed and predictable in the United States
- Most VC documents are drafted for Delaware C-Corps
- Converting from an LLC or other structure later is expensive and creates tax complications

## Founder Stock and 83(b) Elections

When founders receive stock, it is typically subject to vesting (4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff is standard). The IRS treats vesting stock as income, which creates a tax problem: as the stock becomes worth more, founders owe increasing taxes on each vesting tranche [1].

The 83(b) election solves this. By filing an 83(b) election within 30 days of receiving stock, founders pay taxes on the stock's value at the time of grant (usually pennies per share) rather than at the time of vesting (potentially dollars per share). Missing the 83(b) deadline is one of the most expensive mistakes a founder can make [1].

## IP Assignment

All founders must assign their intellectual property to the company. Nathoo warns that failing to do this cleanly creates problems during due diligence for fundraising and acquisitions [1].
